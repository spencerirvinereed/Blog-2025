Here is Matthew 7:13–14 in the original Koine Greek, as found in the Textus Receptus and most standard Greek New Testaments:

📖 Greek (Matthew 7:13–14)

Ματθαῖος 7:13–14

13 Εἰσέλθετε διὰ τῆς στενῆς πύλης· ὅτι πλατεῖα ἡ πύλη καὶ εὐρύχωρος ἡ ὁδὸς ἡ ἀπάγουσα εἰς τὴν ἀπώλειαν, καὶ πολλοί εἰσιν οἱ εἰσερχόμενοι δι’ αὐτῆς·

14 ὅτι στενὴ ἡ πύλη καὶ τεθλιμμένη ἡ ὁδὸς ἡ ἀπάγουσα εἰς τὴν ζωήν, καὶ ὀλίγοι εἰσὶν οἱ εὑρίσκοντες αὐτήν.

Here’s a word-by-word breakdown (parsing) of Matthew 7:13–14 in Greek, focusing on grammatical form and meaning. This will help you understand the structure and nuances of the text.

📖 Matthew 7:13 – Greek Parsing

Εἰσέλθετε

Verb – Aorist Active Imperative, 2nd Person Plural of εἰσέρχομαι

👉 “Enter!”

διὰ

Preposition with Genitive

👉 “through”

τῆς στενῆς πύλης·

  • τῆς – Article, Genitive Feminine Singular

  • στενῆς – Adjective, Genitive Feminine Singular of στενός (“narrow”)

  • πύλης – Noun, Genitive Feminine Singular of πύλη (“gate”)

    👉 “through the narrow gate”

ὅτι

Conjunction

👉 “because” or “for”

πλατεῖα ἡ πύλη

  • πλατεῖα – Adjective, Nominative Feminine Singular of πλατύς (“broad, wide”)

  • ἡ – Article, Nominative Feminine Singular

  • πύλη – Noun, Nominative Feminine Singular

    👉 “wide is the gate”

καὶ εὐρύχωρος ἡ ὁδός

  • καὶ – Conjunction, “and”

  • εὐρύχωρος – Adjective, Nominative Feminine Singular (“spacious, broad”)

  • ἡ – Article

  • ὁδός – Noun, Nominative Feminine Singular (“road, way”)

    👉 “and broad is the way”

ἡ ἀπάγουσα εἰς τὴν ἀπώλειαν

  • ἡ – Article

  • ἀπάγουσα – Participle, Present Active Feminine Singular of ἀπάγω (“to lead away”)

  • εἰς – Preposition with Accusative, “into” or “to”

  • τὴν – Article, Accusative Feminine Singular

  • ἀπώλειαν – Noun, Accusative Feminine Singular (“destruction, ruin”)

    👉 “that leads to destruction”

καὶ πολλοί εἰσιν οἱ εἰσερχόμενοι δι’ αὐτῆς·

  • καὶ – “and”

  • πολλοί – Adjective, Nominative Masculine Plural (“many”)

  • εἰσιν – Verb, Present Active Indicative 3rd Plural of εἰμί (“are”)

  • οἱ εἰσερχόμενοι – Article + Participle, Present Middle/Passive Nominative Masculine Plural of εἰσέρχομαι (“those entering”)

  • δι’ – Preposition (contraction of διά)

  • αὐτῆς – Pronoun, Genitive Feminine Singular (“through it”)

    👉 “and many are those who enter through it”

📖 Matthew 7:14 – Greek Parsing

ὅτι στενὴ ἡ πύλη

  • ὅτι – “because”

  • στενὴ – Adjective, Nominative Feminine Singular (“narrow”)

  • ἡ πύλη – “the gate”

    👉 “because the gate is narrow”

καὶ τεθλιμμένη ἡ ὁδός

  • καὶ – “and”

  • τεθλιμμένη – Perfect Passive Participle, Nominative Feminine Singular of θλίβω (“compressed, afflicted, narrow”)

  • ἡ ὁδός – “the way”

    👉 “and the way is hard/constricted”

ἡ ἀπάγουσα εἰς τὴν ζωήν

  • Same structure as verse 13

    👉 “that leads to life”

καὶ ὀλίγοι εἰσὶν οἱ εὑρίσκοντες αὐτήν.

  • καὶ – “and”

  • ὀλίγοι – Adjective, Nominative Masculine Plural (“few”)

  • εἰσὶν – “are”

  • οἱ εὑρίσκοντες – Article + Present Active Participle, Nominative Masculine Plural of εὑρίσκω (“those finding”)

  • αὐτήν – Pronoun, Accusative Feminine Singular (“it”)

    👉 “and few are those who find it”

🧠 Summary of Key Greek Words

Greek Word Meaning
στενός narrow, constricted
πύλη gate
ὁδός way, path
ἀπώλεια destruction, ruin
ζωή life
ὀλίγοι few
εὑρίσκω to find
θλίβω to press, afflict, constrain
