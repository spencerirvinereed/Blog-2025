Never grasped Einstein’s Theory of Relativity, specifically Special Relativity. Often explained through a tale about an astronaut traveling at near light speed and then returning to Earth.

However, I do know that passage of time feels different and seem relative when a family member is away, serving a mission for example.

To a family with a loved one elsewhere, the duration is exact or seem longer, since the focus is on the individual and are aware of their whereabouts and their doings, through a weekly meet up.

To those outside of immediate circle, the length of their service seem quicker than assigned 18 months or 24 months. They are not informed of the service and not tracking the progress.

Most common responses are,

“Oh are they already coming back, thought they just left”

Open AI Explanation of Special Relativity

The difference in years for an astronaut traveling at near-light speed and then returning to Earth is explained by Einstein’s Theory of Relativity, specifically Special Relativity and the concept of time dilation.

Key Concept: Time Dilation

Time dilation means that time passes at different rates for observers moving relative to each other. According to Einstein’s Special Relativity:

  • As an object moves faster, time slows down for it relative to an observer at rest.

  • The effect becomes dramatic as the object approaches the speed of light.

The Thought Experiment: The Twin Paradox

Imagine a twin astronaut traveling on a spaceship near the speed of light (say, 99.9% of the speed of light) and then returning to Earth.

  1. During the journey:

    • The astronaut’s clock (inside the spaceship) runs much slower compared to a clock on Earth.

    • To the astronaut, time feels normal, but for observers on Earth, the astronaut is aging much more slowly.

  2. When the astronaut returns to Earth:

    • Because time was moving slower for the astronaut, they may have aged only a few years.

    • Meanwhile, much more time has passed on Earth—possibly decades or even centuries.

Why It Happens

  • Space and time are not absolute; they are interconnected in spacetime.

  • High speeds cause space contraction and time dilation, effects predicted and confirmed by relativity.

Conclusion

If an astronaut were to travel at near-light speeds for what feels like a few years for them, they could return to Earth and find that many more years (even centuries) have passed here. This is a real effect and has been experimentally confirmed using high-speed particles and precise atomic clocks.
